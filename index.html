Domain Driven Design

What is it?

Domain-Driven Design shifts focus from
technical expertise to business expertise.

Language

Separation of Concerns

Focus on a domain, rather than
implementation details.

Doesn't focus on

  • What database to use
  • Where will this run?
  • Transport/communication mechanisms
  • etc.

Does focus on

  • Engaging with domain experts
  • Separating concerns into domains and subdomains
  • Model a single domain at a time

Bounded Contexts

Bounded context is a logical boundary
Bounded context tries to define boundaries of our complex domain into business context.

Pros

  • Flexible
  • Works well when business complexity is high
  • Maps to customer's vision of problems/needs
  • Business logic lives in one place (how?)

Cons

  • Doesn't work well when
    technical complexity > business complexity
  • Big committment (how?)
  • Time & Effort
  • Learning curve
